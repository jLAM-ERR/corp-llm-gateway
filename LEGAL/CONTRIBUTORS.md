## Contributor Representation

By submitting a contribution, the contributor confirms that:

1. They have the legal right to submit the contribution.
2. The contribution does not violate obligations to employers,
   clients, or other organizations.
3. The contribution does not include confidential information.
4. The contribution is not copied from incompatible sources.

---

## Employer-Owned Contributions

Contributors must not submit material created within the scope of
their employment duties or under a specific employer assignment
(a "service work", Article 1295 of the Civil Code of the Russian
Federation), created using confidential employer materials, or
subject to third-party ownership restrictions — unless the employer's
written consent (Exhibit A of `LEGAL/CLA.md`), signed by an authorized
representative, is provided to the project owner first.

If a contributor is uncertain whether their employer holds rights in
a contribution, they must resolve that uncertainty before submitting.

---

## Required Agreement

Every commit must carry a sign-off trailer:

```
Signed-off-by: Full Name <email>
```

added automatically by `git commit -s`. Submission of a contribution
with a commit sign-off constitutes acceptance of the Contributor
License Agreement (`LEGAL/CLA.md`). A CI check rejects pull requests
whose commits lack the trailer.

The project owner may additionally request a signed copy of the CLA
at any time and may decline to merge until it is provided.
